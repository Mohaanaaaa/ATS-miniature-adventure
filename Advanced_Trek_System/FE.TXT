This is a very insightful point. In a life-safety system, we have to distinguish between "The Band is moving" and "The Human is alive." 
If a band falls off a trekker's wrist and rolls down a hill, the GPS might show movement, or if it sits still, you might think the person is just resting. 
This is where the Heart Rate (HR) and Sensor Fusion become "Advanced" features.

Here is how we handle this logic to ensure we are tracking a person, not just a plastic device:
1. The "Skin Contact" Validation
Most modern heart rate sensors (like the ones used in ESP32 projects) have a "Signal Quality" or "Contact" lead.

The Logic: If the heart rate sensor returns 0 or a "flatline" value for more than 5 minutes, the system should trigger a "Band Detached" alert.

The Dashboard: On your React map, the marker could turn Gray (meaning "No Biometrics") instead of Blue (Active) or Red (SOS).

2. Sensor Fusion (Motion + Pulse)
We compare the Accelerometer (movement) with the Heart Rate (pulse):
Scenario,GPS/Motion,Heart Rate,System Interpretation

A,Moving,70 - 130 BPM,Normal: Person is trekking.
B,Static,60 - 80 BPM,Resting: Person is taking a break.
C,Moving,0 / No Signal,Warning: Band is lost/falling (Physical movement without a pulse).
D,Static,140+ BPM,Medical Alert: Potential heart distress or panic attack while stationary.

3. The "Alive" Heartbeat Check
To make the system truly reliable, we can add a "Timeout" in your Flask backend.

If the Band stops sending both GPS and HR data, it's a hardware failure or a "Dead Zone."
